Rouse to Hunt

With this amateur-tier Falconry ability, a character can send a trained falcon off to hunt certain kinds of game, for all the purposes that one might go hunting oneself: sport, furs, food, and so on.

A falcon is capable of hunting for ground fowl, waterfowl, or burrowing mammals. This follows the rules for hunting those creatures as given by the Hunting sage ability, save that distance to the target is ignored: if the falcon's attack succeeds (see below), it is assumed to have caught and killed its prey.

When a hunter's first attempt to bag an animal be unsuccessful, if he wishes to keep hunting, he must roll the time required to find new prey. This applies to the falcon, too.

To successfully take its prey, the falcon makes an attack roll (d20+3) against AC 12. A highly-trained falcon, or one of a rare species, may gain a bonus, while unusual prey animals may be be smaller or larger, with higher or lower AC. There are also pieces of equipment and even spells with which a dedicated falconer can further improve his bird's attacks.

A hunting falcon will return to its master after half a hunting day (5 hours) or after making a successful attack, whichever comes first. A victorious falcon will return with its prey intact; a good falconer will let it keep some of the catch as a reward.
