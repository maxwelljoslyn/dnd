$var title: Assassination

To assassinate, a target must be surprised; this is most frequently accomplished through stealth. On a successful attack roll, instead of normal damage, the assassin rolls assassination dice: 2d4-5+X. X is the assassin's level if he is Amateur or higher in the study of Murder; X is 1/2 the assassin's level, rounded down, if he is not.

If the result of the assassination dice is equal to or greater than the target's total HD from race and levels, the target is instantly killed. Otherwise, normal weapon damage is dealt.

If the attack roll to assassinate is a critical hit, the assassin adds 1 less than the damage multiplier to the assassination dice. Thus, a normal (2x) critical adds 1, a 3x critical adds 2, and so on. This is separate from the normal damage increase of a critical hit, which still applies if the assassination dice fail.

Example: Finbolg is an 8th-level assassin with 28 points in Murder, making him an Amateur. He attempts to assassinate and makes his attack roll. His assassination dice are 2d4-5+8 = 2d4+3. A creature with 5 or fewer HD (for instance, a 4th-level character of any playable race) will die no matter the roll, since the minimum on 2d4+3 is 5.
