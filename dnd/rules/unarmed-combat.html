Unarmed Combat

Adapted from rules by Alexis Smolensk.

This study improves a character's ability to fight without weapons. A student of unarmed combat gains improved damage when fighting without weapons, and can employ specialized, more powerful versions of the rules for brawling (wresting/pinning), grabbing (pushing opponents around), and overbearing (attacking such that enemies are forced to fall back.)

At higher tiers of the study, the character can gain skill at savate (foot-fighting), improve their bare-handed attacks to truly deadly levels, and even learn some of the specialized kung-fu kicks and punches ordinarily restricted to monks.

Abilities

Amateur (10-29 points)

  • Strong Grip: reduces chance of being disarmed, when opponent grabs or brawls, from 1/6 to 1/12.
  • Boxing: increases base unarmed damage from 1d4-2 to 1d4.
  • Clinch Fighting: a superior form of brawling which virtually nullifies an opponent's actions.

Authority (30-59 points)

  • Bear Hug: a superior form of brawling which automatically deals crushing damage.
  • Takedown: an improved grab which throws an opponent to the ground.

Expert (61-99 points)
